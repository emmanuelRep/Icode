Meet the Qualifying Teams


Uruguay


The country that hosted the first ever world cup is fighting to reclaim it's lost title as the home of champions. All eyes will be turned to Barcelona star, Luis Suarez who's appearance in the 2010 world cup was nothing short of controversy after sinking his teeth into a player in the opposing team.



Russia


The main hosts of the competition have proven that they are here to stay. Their eyes are on the trophy and they are not looking to let it get on a plane out of Moscow. Will they claim the title for the first time ever? Stay tuned to find out.



Brazil


Home to some of the greatest stars in football's history and a very unique and stylish style of play. Brazil is coming in locked and loaded into the quarter finals. From a country that boasts of names like Neymar, David Luiz, and none other than now retired legend Ronaldinho, you know that the bar is set high for this team.

Will they take it home? or will their shocking defeat back in 2014 come back to plague them.



France


The home of love, tasty baguettes, and the eiffel tower, also has a world cup title to their name. Boasting the likes of Chelsea's N'golo Kante, United's Paul Pogba, and Athletico's Antoine Griezmann. They are coming in with heat and nothing but victory on their mind.
C’est l’histoire de deux pommes de terre. Une d’elles se fait écraser et l’autre s’écrie “Oh purée !”


Croatia


The home of Real Madrid superstar Luka Modric is ready to take on the quarter finals. will they prevail? Guess there is only one way to find out...



Belgium


A team that sits on top of the current FIFA ranking as THE best team in the world, is looking to cement that title with their first ever world cup or continental title.



Sweden


Sweden is one of the most successful teams in the world, having reached four semi finals. The competition is a tough one and the upcoming fixtures are tougher. Will 2018 be the year of the Swedish?



England


Ah yes, the home and birthplace of football. Who would have thought that a game about kicking around pig belly skins filled with air and knocking out the neighbors glass windows would turn into the biggest sport in the world. With the most active and dangerous fans amongst all the teams, England is looking to reclaim their title as the home of football. Will it come home this time?...

FIFA statement on Human Rights Watch report on Russia

FIFA shares Human Rights Watch’s (HRW) objective to ensure decent working conditions on FIFA World Cup stadium construction sites. According to the United Nations Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights, FIFA has a responsibility to use its leverage to ensure the protection of human rights in stadiums that will be used for the FIFA World Cup.

Moscow memories stirred as France, Croatia meet again

Take 15 July 2018, the greatest day in the careers of 23 Frenchmen and for 23 Croatians the absolute worst of memories. Together, they met to contest the Final of the 2018 FIFA World Cup Russia™, Les Bleus defeating Vatreni 4-2 to earn their second star and preventing their rivals from adding a first to their chequered shirts.

The meteoric rise of Kylian Mbappe

Kylian Mbappe illuminated the 2018 FIFA World Cup Russia™. He produced a breathtaking performance to down Argentina in the Round of 16, scored a super goal – his fourth of the tournament – in the 4-2 victory over Croatia in the Final, and won the FIFA Young Player award.
